Hi, I'm Chenyan Jing

Researcher in Surgical AI and Robotic Perception

I recently completed my M.S.E. in Robotics at Johns Hopkins University. My research lies at the intersection of computer vision, surgical AI, and robotic perception. I am interested in building perception and world models that help intelligent systems understand surgical scenes, reason about tool–tissue interactions, and support safe robotic autonomy.

Email CV Google Scholar GitHub

About

My work focuses on visual understanding for medical and robotic systems, including functional landmark localization, dense tissue affordance prediction, temporal modeling, depth-aware perception, and generative world models for surgical video.

More broadly, I am interested in how structured visual representations can connect perception, prediction, and action in complex real-world environments.
